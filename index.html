About Us

Over 70 percent of Americans don't trust news to be reported fairly and accurately.

The current methods for finding news, including social media and news aggregators like Apple News or Google News, reinforce filter bubbles that return news similar to what readers search for and have reach in the past.

newsIT Diversifies Everyones News Profile

Our app pulls articles from over 30,000 different sources.

See the breadth of opinions on a topic in a single page.

newsIT sorts articles based on sentiment text analysis, showing readers the full breadth of opinion on a topic, outlined in a single search result.

Analyze News Trends

newsIT professional users can save snapshots of senitment results, and see how sentiment changes on a topic overtime.

newsIT solves problems of research and curation in a single, elegant platform.

Casual users can rely on newsIT to provide a more diversified news feed than they might using existing platforms, making for a more varied and enticing reading experience.

Heavy users, like news researchers, editors, journalists and academic users, can use newsIT filtering and metrics to quickly dive into political polarization.

newsIT uses objective metrics to determine diversity in thought.

newsIT's sentiment analysis breaks down articles based on the disposition of the author on a topic. Articles are then sorted based on normalized sentiment scores, such that the most enthusiastic or positive article and the most skeptical or negative article surface.

Sentiment is a neutral measurement of the density of certain qualifiers within the text.

newsIT runs autonomously and collects the latest breaking articles on any given topic. There is no need for human intervention or subjective curation.
